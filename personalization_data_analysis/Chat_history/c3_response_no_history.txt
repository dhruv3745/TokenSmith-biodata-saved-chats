The optimizer decides between a full table scan and an index range scan based on the selectivity of the predicate and the size of the table. If 
the predicate is highly selective, meaning that only a small fraction of the rows in the table satisfy the predicate, then an index range scan  
is likely to be more efficient than a full table scan. On the other hand, if the predicate is moderately selective, meaning that a significant  
fraction of the rows in the table satisfy the predicate, then a full table scan may be more efficient than an index range scan. The optimizer   
uses statistics about the table, such as the number of rows and the distribution of values in the table, to determine the selectivity of the    
predicate and to make this decision. The specific criteria and mechanisms for this decision are complex and are described in the bibliographic  
notes for this chapter.
