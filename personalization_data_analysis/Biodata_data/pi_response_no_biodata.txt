A hash join works by partitioning both input relations into smaller pieces such that each partition of the smaller relation fits into memory.   
The tuples in the partition of the build relation are retained in memory, and an in-memory index is built on these tuples. When the probe       
relation tuples arrive at the node, they are also repartitioned; tuples in the partition are used directly to probe the index on the tuples in  
the partition, instead of being written out to disk and read back in.
